Leopard

The leopard (Panthera pardus) is a member of the Felidae family and the smallest of the four "big cats," the other three being the tiger, lion and jaguar.

  • Scientific Name:Panthera pardus
  • Average Length: 11centimeter
  • Average Lifespan:12 to 17 years
  • Habitat:sub-Saharan Africa and southern Asia.

Leopards are big cats known for their golden, spotted bodies and graceful, yet ferocious hunting techniques.Their skulls are notably large and their jaws are so powerful that they can take prey much larger than themselves.The leopard grows to over 6 feet in length, excluding its over 4 foot tail, and up to 176 lbs. in weight.Its coat ranges from tawny to yellow in color with numerous dark,rosette-shaped markings which vary based on subspecies and region.
